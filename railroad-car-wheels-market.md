<p><p>The railroad car wheels market features two primary types: monoblock wheels and strake wheels. Monoblock wheels are single-piece designs that offer durability and lower maintenance, making them suitable for heavy freight transport. Strake wheels, on the other hand, have a modified profile with ribs or 'strakes' to enhance traction and performance, especially in varied track conditions. Both types cater to different operational needs, contributing to safety and efficiency in rail transportation.</p></p>
