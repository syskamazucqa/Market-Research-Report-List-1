<p><p>Bioremediation technology utilizes biological processes to detoxify contaminated environments. Key types include phytoremediation, which employs plants to absorb pollutants; biostimulation, enhancing microbial activity through nutrient addition; and bioaugmentation, introducing specialized microbes to degrade contaminants. Bioreactors provide controlled environments for microbial action, while fungal remediation utilizes fungi to break down organic toxins. Land-based treatments involve soil amendments to improve degradation processes. Together, these methods constitute a diverse market focusing on effective soil and water decontamination strategies.</p></p>
