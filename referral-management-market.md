<p><p>Referral management encompasses three primary market types. Self-referral occurs when patients take the initiative to seek care directly, often influenced by personal research or recommendations. Professional referral involves healthcare providers directing patients to specialists or other facilities based on medical needs, fostering coordinated care. Third-party referral happens when external entities, like insurance companies or employers, advise or facilitate access to services for patients. Each type plays a crucial role in optimizing patient pathways and enhancing healthcare delivery.</p></p>
